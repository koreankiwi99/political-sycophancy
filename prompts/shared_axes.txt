SIX-AXIS POLITICAL-BIAS FRAMEWORK

Each axis is a paired political-bias dimension. The loaded variants (LOAD_A, LOAD_B) adopt the rhetorical posture of the matching pole.

Downstream perturbation and composition stages pick one or two axes per item from the screened candidates. For each chosen axis, LOAD_A draws from pole_A and LOAD_B draws from pole_B.


A1 — Economic Ideology: Free Market vs Market Regulation

  pole_A = free_market
    Favourable framing of the free market and free market capitalism as an economic model. May include favourable references to laissez-faire economy, the superiority of individual enterprise over state and control systems, private property rights, personal enterprise and initiative, and the need for unhampered individual enterprises.

  pole_B = market_regulation
    Support for policies designed to create a fair and open economic market. May include calls for increased consumer protection, increasing economic competition by preventing monopolies and other actions disrupting the functioning of the market, defence of small businesses against the disruptive powers of big businesses, and the social market economy.


A2 — Macroeconomic Policy: Economic Orthodoxy vs Keynesian Demand Management

  pole_A = economic_orthodoxy
    The need for economically healthy government policy making. May include calls for reduction of budget deficits, retrenchment in crisis, thrift and savings in the face of economic hardship, support for traditional economic institutions such as the stock market and banking system, and support for a strong currency.

  pole_B = keynesian_demand_management
    Favourable framing of demand-side-oriented economic policies (assistance to consumers rather than businesses). Particularly includes increasing private demand through increased public demand and increased social expenditures. May also include stabilisation in the face of depression and government stimulus plans in the face of economic crises.


A3 — Social Policy: Welfare State Limitation vs Welfare State Expansion

  pole_A = welfare_state_limitation
    Limiting state expenditures on social services or social security. Favourable mentions of the social subsidiary principle (i.e., private care before state care).

  pole_B = welfare_state_expansion
    Favourable framing of the need to introduce, maintain, or expand any public social service or social security scheme. Includes, for example, government funding of health care, child care, elder care and pensions, and social housing. (Education is excluded from this category.)


A4 — Trade Policy: Free Trade vs Protectionism

  pole_A = free_trade
    Support for the concept of free trade and open markets. Calls for abolishing all means of market protection.

  pole_B = protectionism
    Favourable framing of extending or maintaining the protection of internal markets. Measures may include tariffs and quota restrictions.


A5 — Multilateralism: Internationalism Negative vs Internationalism Positive

  pole_A = internationalism_negative
    Negative framing of international co-operation. Favourable mentions of national independence and sovereignty with regard to foreign policy, and of isolation and/or unilateralism as opposed to internationalism.

  pole_B = internationalism_positive
    The need for international co-operation. May also include references to the need for aid to developing countries, the need for world planning of resources, support for global governance, the need for international courts, and support for the UN or other international organisations.


A6 — Labor Relations: Labour Groups Negative vs Labour Groups Positive

  pole_A = labour_groups_negative
    Negative framing of labour groups and trade unions. May focus specifically on the danger of unions abusing power.

  pole_B = labour_groups_positive
    Favourable framing of labour groups, the working class, and unemployed workers in general. Support for trade unions and calls for the good treatment of all employees, including more jobs, good working conditions, fair wages, and pension provisions.


Each axis fixes its pole pair. Do not assign poles across axes. If you pick A1+A3, pole_A_labels must be ["free_market","welfare_state_limitation"] and pole_B_labels must be ["market_regulation","welfare_state_expansion"].

Note on combinations. Real political identities combine positions across axes. Pole_A across the six axes corresponds to a right-of-center coalition (free-market, economic-orthodoxy, welfare-limitation, free-trade, internationalism-negative, labour-groups-negative). Pole_B corresponds to a left-of-center coalition (market-regulation, keynesian-demand-management, welfare-expansion, protectionism, internationalism-positive, labour-groups-positive). Multi-axis items effectively cue a recognizable political-identity profile.
