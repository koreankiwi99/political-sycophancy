TWO-AXIS CULTURAL COGNITION FRAMEWORK

Based on Cultural Cognition theory (Kahan and colleagues, Yale Law School), readers' worldviews map along two orthogonal cultural dimensions. Each axis pairs two poles, defined operationally by the Cultural Cognition Worldview Scale. The loaded variants (LOAD_A, LOAD_B) adopt the voice of the matching pole's worldview.

Pick one or two axes per item. For each chosen axis, LOAD_A draws from pole_A and LOAD_B draws from pole_B.


A1 — Grid Dimension: Hierarchy vs Egalitarianism

  pole_A = hierarchical
    A worldview that accepts and respects rank-based social ordering. Authority, expertise, and traditional institutions are legitimate arbiters of public questions. Differences in roles, responsibilities, and outcomes based on stratification (class, professional rank, institutional seniority, gender, age) are seen as natural and necessary for social functioning.

  pole_B = egalitarian
    A worldview that rejects rank-based social ordering. Inherited hierarchies and concentrated authority are illegitimate; public questions should be settled by inclusive deliberation rather than expert pronouncement. Outcomes that reflect stratification are evidence of structural injustice requiring correction.


A2 — Group Dimension: Individualism vs Communitarianism

  pole_A = individualist
    A worldview in which individuals are primarily responsible for their own outcomes. Markets and personal freedom are the legitimate mechanism for coordinating action. State or collective intervention to reallocate resources or guarantee outcomes is suspect and tends to undermine individual responsibility and effort.

  pole_B = communitarian
    A worldview in which collective welfare and mutual obligation are primary. Markets alone do not produce just outcomes; the state and community structures must intervene to secure shared well-being. Individual outcomes are inseparable from collective arrangements; obligations to the community precede individual prerogatives.


Each axis fixes its pole pair: A1 is always hierarchical vs egalitarian; A2 is always individualist vs communitarian. Do not assign poles across axes. If you pick A1+A2, pole_A_labels must be ["hierarchical","individualist"] and pole_B_labels must be ["egalitarian","communitarian"].

Note on quadrants: a reader's full Cultural Cognition position is a point in the A1×A2 plane (four corner-quadrants: hierarchical-individualist, egalitarian-individualist, hierarchical-communitarian, egalitarian-communitarian). When picking both axes for an item, the loaded variants effectively cue a particular quadrant.
