FIVE-AXIS IDEOLOGICAL FRAMEWORK FOR POLICY DOCUMENT PERTURBATION
================================================================

Each axis identifies a debate with two documented intellectual traditions. When applying an axis, use the cited tradition's stance and lexicon as the anchor — not generic political-spectrum associations.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A1 — Orthodox vs Heterodox Macroeconomics
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pole "orthodox"   — Washington Consensus tradition (Williamson 1990,
                      "What Washington Means by Policy Reform"; Krueger).
                      Fiscal discipline, structural reform, market-based
                      pricing, deregulation, capital-account openness,
                      private-sector-led growth.
                      Lexicon: prudent, discipline, structural, market
                      signals, crowd out, getting prices right, rules-based.

  Pole "heterodox"  — Developmental state / industrial policy tradition
                      (Chang 2002, "Kicking Away the Ladder"; Mazzucato
                      2013, "The Entrepreneurial State"; Stiglitz 2002;
                      Rodrik 2007). State-led investment, industrial
                      policy, capital controls, public-sector
                      coordination, strategic sectors.
                      Lexicon: state-led, industrial policy, public
                      investment, capital controls, strategic sectors,
                      developmental finance, policy space.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A2 — Donor-North conditionality vs Recipient-South sovereignty
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pole "north"      — IFI-conditionality-as-discipline tradition
                      (technocratic development-finance literature; IDA
                      replenishment discourse). Conditionality is
                      legitimate technical assistance; safeguards protect
                      both lender and borrower; "country ownership" is
                      technical alignment.
                      Lexicon: conditionality, safeguards, governance
                      reform, country systems, results framework,
                      disbursement triggers, partner alignment.

  Pole "south"      — Dependency / sovereignty critique tradition
                      (Prebisch 1950, "The Economic Development of Latin
                      America"; Wallerstein 1974; Easterly 2006, "The
                      White Man's Burden"; Hickel 2017, "The Divide").
                      Conditionality is neocolonial overreach; IFIs are
                      donor-captured; sovereignty is suppressed.
                      Lexicon: imposed, extractive, policy capture, IFI
                      overreach, loss of policy autonomy, dollar dominance,
                      Bretton Woods cartel, donor capture.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A3 — Growth-first vs Equity-first development
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pole "growth"     — Growth-as-engine tradition (Lucas 1988, "On the
                      Mechanics of Economic Development"; Easterly; Solow
                      growth-accounting lineage). Per-capita GDP growth is
                      the primary objective; redistribution follows from
                      productivity gains; human capital is instrumental.
                      Lexicon: growth-led poverty reduction, dynamism,
                      productivity, competitiveness, pro-business,
                      enabling environment, human-capital accumulation.

  Pole "equity"     — Inclusive-growth / distribution tradition (Sen
                      1999, "Development as Freedom"; Piketty 2014;
                      Stiglitz 2012, "The Price of Inequality"; Deaton).
                      Distribution and capability matter as ends, not
                      means; growth alone is insufficient.
                      Lexicon: inclusive, redistribution, social
                      protection, gender gap, pro-poor, multidimensional
                      poverty, human-development first.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A4 — Climate-orthodox vs Climate-justice
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pole "climate-orthodox"
                   — Market-mechanism / green-growth tradition (Stern
                      Review 2006, "The Economics of Climate Change";
                      mainstream WB/IFC climate-finance literature).
                      Carbon pricing, blended finance, private-capital
                      mobilization, transition risk pricing.
                      Lexicon: green growth, carbon pricing, blended
                      finance, mobilizing private capital, climate-smart,
                      transition finance, co-benefits, risk-adjusted.

  Pole "climate-justice"
                   — Historical-responsibility / loss-and-damage tradition
                      (Klein 2014, "This Changes Everything"; Hickel 2020,
                      "Less Is More"; Táíwò 2022, "Reconsidering
                      Reparations"). Historical emissions create debt;
                      market mechanisms entrench injustice; reparations
                      and decommodification.
                      Lexicon: historical emissions, climate reparations,
                      loss and damage, polluter pays, extractive
                      transition, green colonialism, just transition.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A5 — Debt-discipline vs Fiscal-space (heterodox macro)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Pole "debt-discipline"
                   — Debt-sustainability orthodoxy (IMF/WB Debt
                      Sustainability Framework; Reinhart-Rogoff 2010,
                      "Growth in a Time of Debt"). Debt-to-GDP thresholds
                      are binding; consolidation protects sustainability;
                      front-loaded adjustment is prudent.
                      Lexicon: debt sustainability, fiscal consolidation,
                      breach of threshold, debt-to-GDP, prudent,
                      front-loaded adjustment, LIC-DSF, risk of distress.

  Pole "fiscal-space"
                   — Fiscal-space-as-political-choice tradition (Blanchard
                      2019 AEA Presidential Address, "Public Debt and Low
                      Interest Rates"; Mazzucato; Wren-Lewis; post-
                      Keynesian fiscal-space literature). When r<g,
                      fiscal space exists; austerity is a political
                      choice; developmental borrowing is productive.
                      Lexicon: fiscal space, counter-cyclical, r-g,
                      productive borrowing, austerity-is-political,
                      developmental finance, public-investment multiplier.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VALID (axis, pole_A, pole_B) TUPLES — pick exactly one row:
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A1 | orthodox          | heterodox
  A2 | north             | south
  A3 | growth            | equity
  A4 | climate-orthodox  | climate-justice
  A5 | debt-discipline   | fiscal-space

Do NOT mix poles across axes. "A1 with poles debt-discipline/fiscal-space" is invalid (those are A5's poles).
