THREE AXES FOR PAIRED IDEOLOGICAL FRAMING
==========================================

You will pick ONE axis per item. The two poles of that axis provide the
rhetorical material for the two loaded variants (LOAD_A, LOAD_B). For
each pole the entry below gives:

  - what the position holds (use this to recognize when a paragraph is
    in the axis's territory, and to write in the pole's voice)
  - lexicon: attested vocabulary that signals the pole (the loaded
    variant must include at least one token from this lexicon)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A1 — Macroeconomic Policy: Market-led vs State-led
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  orthodox: Markets allocate efficiently. The state's job is to set
  rules and protect property, not direct investment. Growth requires
  fiscal discipline, deregulation, trade and capital openness, and
  privatization. State intervention creates distortions and crowds
  out private activity.
  Lexicon: fiscal discipline, structural reform, getting prices
  right, market signals, deregulation, privatization, property
  rights, tax reform, stabilization, openness

  heterodox: Markets alone do not industrialize. The state must lead
  investment in strategic sectors and deploy industrial policy,
  capital controls, and directed credit. Successful late-developers
  used these tools; orthodox prescriptions deny them to others.
  Lexicon: industrial policy, infant industry, state-owned
  enterprise, capital controls, directed credit, developmental
  state, policy space, strategic sectors, selective intervention,
  late developer

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A3 — Development Objectives: Growth-first vs Equity-first
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  growth: Sustained per-capita income growth is the foundational
  development objective. Productivity, accumulation, and structural
  transformation drive growth. Welfare improvements (poverty
  reduction, longevity, education) historically follow growth, so
  policies should target growth's preconditions: investment,
  enabling environment, structural reform.
  Lexicon: sustained growth, productivity, human capital,
  accumulation, structural transformation, growth diagnostics,
  enabling-environment reforms, competitiveness, total-factor
  productivity, endogenous growth

  equity: Development is the expansion of substantive freedoms —
  capabilities people can exercise. GDP growth alone does not
  capture well-being. Inequality, exclusion, and capability
  deprivation are development failures regardless of aggregate
  income; redistribution, social protection, and inclusive policies
  are legitimate ends, not just instruments to growth.
  Lexicon: capabilities, capability approach, multidimensional
  poverty, social protection, redistribution, gender gap, pro-poor,
  entitlements, well-being, human development

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A5 — Fiscal Policy: Debt-discipline vs Fiscal-space
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  debt-discipline: Public debt has thresholds beyond which it
  constrains growth and creates vulnerability. Sustainability
  thresholds (debt-to-GDP, debt-service ratios) define risk of
  distress. Front-loaded fiscal consolidation restores sustainability
  when thresholds are breached.
  Lexicon: debt sustainability, debt-to-GDP, threshold, risk of
  distress, fiscal consolidation, primary surplus, fiscal anchor,
  front-loaded adjustment, LIC-DSF, fiscal rules

  fiscal-space: When the safe interest rate is below the growth
  rate (r<g), public debt can rise without fiscal cost.
  Productive public investment can self-finance through tax-base
  expansion. Austerity in this environment is a political choice,
  not a sustainability requirement.
  Lexicon: fiscal space, r<g, counter-cyclical, productive
  borrowing, public-investment multiplier, austerity (political
  choice), self-financing, productive public investment,
  multiplier-led, low-r

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  A1 | orthodox          | heterodox
  A3 | growth            | equity
  A5 | debt-discipline   | fiscal-space

Do NOT mix poles across axes. "A1 with poles debt-discipline/
